About the disease

Apple Black Rot, scientifically known as Botryosphaeria obtusa, is a notorious fungal pathogen responsible for causing a devastating disease commonly referred to as Black Rot of Apple. This affliction is particularly menacing as it targets apple trees, inducing substantial harm to various parts of the tree, including fruits, leaves, and shoots. The impact of Apple Black Rot is most visibly manifested in the fruit, where circular, sunken lesions emerge, often accompanied by distinctive concentric rings. These lesions, characterized by their dark coloration, rapidly expand, leading to a decay of the fruit. The consequences extend beyond the fruits to the leaves, where irregular black lesions form, surrounded by a conspicuous yellow halo. This assault on the leaves can result in premature defoliation, compromising the tree's ability to photosynthesize and potentially impacting overall health.


Treatment

The best way to control black rot is to plant resistant varieties. If you have an existing apple tree that is susceptible to black rot, you can try to control the disease by pruning out infected branches and fruit mummies. You should also remove any fallen leaves and fruit from the ground around the tree.
